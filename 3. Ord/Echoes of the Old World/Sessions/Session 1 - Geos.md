[[Geos]] plotline starts. The land goes from wintry, cold and damp and becomes suddenly lush and green, with animals and birds flitting around the undergrowth. An Old Place rises to the southwest, imposing towers of green dominating the landscape. 

Should the players choose to venture into the Old Place, the undergrowth becomes more and more thick, and only small saplings rise from this brush. However, gigantic towers completely coating in flowering climbing plants rise around 60 feet apart on a regular grid. Flying squirrels soar on thermals and wind rushes through the boulevards; foxes and birds of prey nest in the walls of the towers. The ground is warm and damp with fresh rainfall. 

Insight check DC13: anyone who succeeds notices the feeling of being watched. This feeling persists as they venture deeper into the Old Place and the brush. 

Once they reach the central square or turn around, they find a humanoid shape forms behind them from the vines and creepers coating the ground. This is Geos' avatar. At this point they only feel watched from the avatar. Geos speaks to the party, explaining that they need help and inviting them to a safe resting place. The vines in a nearby tower part to reveal an organic-looking wooden structure with an opening revealing a dimly-lit room. 

The room is approx. 20x20 feet with a wooden floor and walls, gently illuminated by bioluminescent flowers. It is richly furnished with furniture and tapestries, rugs and a fireplace. On closer inspection, however the tapestries are part of the walls, the rugs the floor; the furniture melds smoothly into the fabric of the room, and in the fireplace is simply bioluminescent flowers blowing in a gentle breeze. It is pleasantly warm in here, slightly cooler than the humid heat of outside. The figure is seated in one of the comfortable-looking armchairs. There are four more seats available, a narrow, high-backed chair built for an armoured figure, with crescent patterns shimmering as if pressed into the surface; a squat, rounded-looking one with a hefty hook on the side; a high-backed one with four headrests; and an ottoman decorated with hundreds of different animals in the grain of the wood. In the centre is a map of a city on a series of pillars.

The figure gestures for them to sit. If they do, it explains it is named Geos, and it is very happy to see you. 

If they do, it insists they do. (Remind them they have been on the road for days and it is warm and dry and comfortable in here). If they say no again, Geos offers them repast and rest for as long as the night, in exchange for conversation with a lonely soul. Geos appears honest in this desire. If they still say no, Geos will start his story and follow them as they leave.

Geos speaks of their past, their original as the memories of the leader of this city one thousand thousand years ago. He volunteered for an experiment which was conceived to upload his consciousness to the floral lifeweb in this city. His memories would be added to by the memories of any future leaders who volunteered, to add to the expertise and aid in running the city. At its height, the 99th leader controlled most of East Antar. Then the empire collapsed. It was a dark time, [[Geos]] says, and seems in deep thought.

They answer any questions the PCs may have, and they speak of many things long into the night. Geos is warm and genial, and they get along well. 

Just as they are about to turn in, Geos turns to Valentine and speaks.

> Valentine, I must tell you something. I sensed you returning to this place. I can sense you because you are part of me, part of the lifeweb. It is what gives you your abilities. Many years ago now, I gave shelter to a young huntress. After we spoke she would often return here to shelter from, well, a somewhat troubled home life. One day she turned up with child. I sensed an opportunity to both aid her and myself. I gave you a piece of myself, Valentine. 
> 
> Now the real question is - which are you?

At this point he speaks three names quickly:

> Farah; Sinda; Plemnos!

He waits, silent for a moment. Insight 9 reveals he is disappointed to have seen no effect, like he was expecting something to happen in response. Then they murmur, "Interesting...". 

They seem to consider Valentine anew, then they say

> I make you an offer, Valentine. Join me, and together we will revitalise this city and lead it to new heights. 

When Valentine declines, Geos will say

> Well then. We are at cross-purposes, it would seem. Sleep on it, perhaps. I have not long to find an heir, dying as I am.
