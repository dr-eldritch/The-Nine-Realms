Enter into more mountainous environment. See a castle atop the valley. Come to a river crossing, with the first proper settlement since setting off, a town of around 200. Stay in the town or continue to the castle first?

There are people of all sorts out and about, shopping at a market, hanging out washing, digging gardens, and mending thatched roofs. The river Mymet powers a great water wheel at the edge of town which is used to charge rotary energy storage devices known as Whirligigs (found in a giant ceramic machine which is similar to a giant strip-mining machine like Bagger 288 crossed with a boring drill). The Whirligigs store energy while their rune is inactive, and release the rotational energy when the rune is activated. It seems to be the same sort of energy-storing rune magic as the Hearthstone.

The travellers will be greeted at the inn by the innkeeper and town coordinator. They will welcome them to the town of Damf-upon-Mymet. The innkeeper will tell them of a bug infestation plaguing their town's mines. Under the town is another Old Place, this time an ancient palace complex. The Whirligigs were found at a nearby site and have been extremely useful in mining the palace for materials and magic. However, the insectoid infestation is causing them trouble, both killing their miners and somehow suppressing the Whirligigs' runes. (This means that when a bug is killed, a Whirligig will start up.)

Recently, the bugs have become even more aggressive and are pushing to the surface. Around the same time, miners and guards began to run away from town suddenly, abandoning their families and posts.

In the heart of the palace-mine is a rift in the world, a metastable tear in reality caused by an undying insectoid queen bridging the gap between life and the Deepness. The insectoids are protective drones and will fight to defend the queen. The palace has become the home of this queen, her hive for many years before the ritual which made her undead. The ritual also shook the ground enough to reveal the caves which allowed the town of Damf-upon-Mymet to access the palace *via* their existing mineshafts and caves, where they initially found the Whirligigs .
