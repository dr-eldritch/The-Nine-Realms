It is the night of Sirimus in the small Antari town of Brent. There is drinking and eating and celebration, before the sun dips below the horizon for a full month for Aetervek. 

As the sun fully sets, any refraction being cloaked by the Deepness, a flash from the south. Moments later a shockwave races across the landscape from the south, accompanied by a deep boom.

All at once the Deepness boils in the sky, and rushes towards Siri, the Lodestar. They seem to tussle, briefly, until Siri winks out unceremoniously. The Deepness, that chaotic, unpredictable cloak of oil, now blankets the entire sky. 

The world holds its breath. Nobody moves. Then a scream cuts across the night air. 

The air appears to wobble, as if a strong heat haze is present. Then a rent appears in the very fabric of reality, and a creature of abstract eldritch terror step out, so incomprehensible that the mind reels. It seems to snatch at a villager, who screams in terror and pain, but is otherwise unharmed. The creature pauses, seeming to reassess. This gives the rest of the village a chance to run, but by this point more and more tears in reality are opening up, birthing forth more and varied horrors.

The horrors do psychic damage only, seemingly unable to make physical contact with anything. If the party fights back effectively, the horrors will get weaker round on round, eventually retreating back into their rents. 

The village is in disarray, at least a dozen people lie dead with no visible injuries aside from bloodshot eyes.

The party must venture forth and find out what happened, and why.
