Practitioners of arcane magic (arcanists) hold artefacts as their power source, tapping into the ancient ley lines and shaping their power in specific ways. Arcane mages must prepare specific artefacts or 'spells' which are used up and must recharge. For example, a lapidary might have a gauntlet with slots for gemstones which can be swapped out on a rest. A cartomancer might prepare a deck of specific spells each rest. A wizard might write their spells in a book, rewriting those which are used up and need to be swapped. 

Once an arcanist learns a spell, they cannot forget it - when it is used up, this is only until they next spend time to re-prepare their spells.

Each spell uses a preparation slot, with higher level spells using up their level in slots, and higher level PCs getting more slots.

Arcane practitioners may use a catalyst but the source of the change comes from their own mind.
