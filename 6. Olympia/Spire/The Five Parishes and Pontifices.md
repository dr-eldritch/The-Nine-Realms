The city is made up of five Parishes, each governed by a Pontifex, with the fifth governed by the ArchPontifex. The Pontifices are of equal rank, but are ruled over by the ArchPontifex. The Solari Pontificery is the celestial government, forming a classic theocracy, with the Solars as the eponymous gods. The Pontifices therefore are not themselves Solars, but use the Solars to justify their governance.

There is the Parochia Abgrundi at the bottom, governed by Pontifex Arcanum. His power comes from the arcane harvesters of the EM at the base of Spire. [means of production] (wand)

There is the Parochia Obscuri under the smog, governed by Pontifex Umbra. Her power comes from ignorance. The more people believe her to be powerful, the more powerful she is. She runs very successful propaganda campaigns in the Parish. [open knowledge] (amulet)

There is the Parochia Communi in the middle, governed by Pontifex Populi. Their power comes from life tithes extorted or forcibly extracted at the factories using arcane devices known as Tolltakers. In order to go to the factory to work, the workers must pay the life tithe. [collective action] (figurine)

There is the Parochia Vacari above the atmosphere, governed by Pontifex Nemo. Their power comes from wealth; the more they own, the greater their influence - and they own half of Spire (though not the factories). [collective ownership] (coin)

There is the Parochia Solari at the very top, governed by ArchPontifex Stella. Her power comes from the Solars, siphoning their magic to be her own. If this siphon can be broken, the gods will return in their former glory. This siphon is not a device but a contract the Solars were tricked into signing. If it can be destroyed, the Solars return to power. [social contract] (emerald quill)

------------------------------------

The Pontifices each have different sources of power within the city, which will have to be discovered and taken out in order to defeat them. They keep the sources highly secret, distributed and guarded to prevent sabotage.

The Theocracy claims the Solars are demigods, but in reality they hold little actual magical power, sitting as puppets of the Pontifices, whose power comes from the city of Spire itself. Thousands of years ago, the ArchPontifex discovered a way to siphon the Solars' power and took over governance of Spire, relegating the Solars to figureheads and claiming that they had retired from government, instead choosing to rule from afar through the Pontifices.

If the Solars are restored to their former glory, they will be grateful enough to make those who helped into demigods themselves. However, they may also choose to write up a new contract, binding the Solars to acts of equity and collective ownership, action and knowledge. Those who helped must choose between their own reward, and restoring the people of Spire.
