Intro Box Text:
	Spire is the ancient seat of the Solars, the celestials which control Olympia.  In Spire, as in Olympia as a whole, there are two classes: celestials, and everyone else.
	Spire as viewed from the sky is, as its name suggests, an unfathomably large needle pointing to the heavens. At the vacuum-piercing tip of the needle live the Solars, ancient celestial demigods who control all laws, including strict rules surrounding magic use in the city. 
	As we travel down from the needlepoint, we see the lavish houses and temples of the Void District encircling the narrow structure, getting less expansive and expensive as we descend. The needle thickens, and soon we reach the bustling plazas and packed houses of the Commune, then as the haze of Olympia's atmosphere begins to obscure the view we see industry and poverty intertwined as we spiral down. 
	Below here is Smogtown, invisible through the fumes of the factories which have fallen to smother the city. Beneath this point is the Immersea, wherein the gaseous atmosphere of Olympia thickens to a point where specialised Kin may swim as if underwater. And at the very base of Spire is the Avgrund, the base of the needle, a mystery to all except those who control it - the Solars.
	The Solars call this the City of Peace, call Olympia the Plane of Peace. But it is here unrest is being fomented. You have been hired by a mysterious figure known only as The Spider to carry out a job which you know will help destabilise the Solari government. You all have your own reasons for doing this; maybe you were wronged by the Solars; perhaps your reasoning is more philosophical - you think magic should be for all, or that the current system is unjust. Regardless, you must be careful. Agents of the Solars are everywhere, watching, waiting for treasonous behaviour to make itself known.
	So welcome to Spire, the Unfellable City.


(The structure of Spire is a space elevator travelling from the surface of Olympia deep in the depths of the gas giant's core, all the way to the vacuum of space. It is the second-largest megastructure in the System, after the Rust Belt (though most Solars would say that doesn't count any more).)
